Topics of Interest

Topics of interest are listed below. List is not exhaustive.

  • Requirements elicitation;
  • Requirements management and traceability;
  • Requirements negotiation, compatibility, and prioritization;
  • Requirements validation and verification;
  • Requirements engineering education and training;
  • Requirements management within specific paradigms, e.g., agent-oriented, model-driven, agile, or service-oriented;
  • Requirements management in times of pandemic;
  • Requirements management in ubiquitous computing, e.g., cyber-physical systems, ecosystems of things, extended reality;
  • Development of domain-specific languages, formalization, and meta-modeling;
  • Customization of requirements management tools;
  • Support for legal aspects, certifications, and intellectual property.
  • Generative AI and Large Language Models (LLMs) for Requirements Engineering
  • AI-aided Requirements elicitation, analysis, and validation
  • Smart assistants for Requirements Engineering
  • Task automation in Requirements Engineering aided by generative AI

Submission Guidelines

The Software Requirement Tools Track (WER-SRTT) offers researchers and professionals a dedicated space to introduce and showcase tools that support any phase of Software Requirements Engineering. This track includes both technical and practical presentations, with live demos of the tools highlighting their features and real-world applications.

Researchers are encouraged to submit tools from applied research, while industry professionals are invited to present commercial or proprietary tools that have led to improvements in productivity or quality in Requirements Engineering.

Submissions must include a written paper and a video demonstration, adhering to the following guidelines:

Paper

  • The submission must be original, unpublished work, and must not be under review at any other event or scientific journal.
  • The contents must include a clear account of:
    • its motivation, or the problem the tool intends to solve
    • its main features and target audience
    • use examples and screenshots (if applicable)
    • its architecture and main components, as well as a description of its interface
    • a brief comparison with similar tools
    • its licencing policy (e.g., open-source or proprietary)
  • The document must be 5 to 8 pages in extension following LNCS format

Video

  • A 3 to 6 minute video demonstration displaying the tool at work, narrated in the same language as the paper
  • The video must be informative and visually demonstrate the main features and benefits of the tool.

Submission and Evaluation Process

All submissions that include both article and video will be evaluated by at least three committee members. Submissions that do not include the required combination of article and video, are outside the scope, or violate the originality criteria will be desk-rejected, and the chairs of related tracks will be notified.

Authors are encouraged to provide links to open source code and datasets in preserved digital repositories (e.g., zenodo.org, figshare.com, softwareheritage.org). For proprietary tools, authors must justify why they are not making the code available.

Evaluation Criteria

  • Relevance of the tool for Software Requirements Engineering
  • Innovation and contribution in relation to existing tools
  • Clarity and quality of the written and video presentation
  • Relationship with related works in the area
